Learning Experience Design (LXD) — Job-Ready Crash Course

Your essential one-page education for landing an entry-level role

What is LXD?

Learning Experience Design combines instructional design, user experience (UX), and learning science to create effective, engaging, and measurable learning solutions. As an LXD, you design and develop experiences that help people acquire skills and knowledge efficiently.

Key Skills to Learn

  • Writing measurable learning objectives using Bloom’s Taxonomy.
  • Applying instructional design models (ADDIE, SAM, Backward Design).
  • Understanding learning theories (constructivism, behaviorism, andragogy).
  • Designing accessible content (WCAG standards, alt text, captions).
  • Creating engaging learning materials (videos, eLearning modules, job aids).
  • Using authoring tools (Articulate Storyline, Rise, H5P).
  • Managing projects and collaborating with SMEs.

Essential Tools

  • Authoring Tools: Articulate Storyline/Rise, Adobe Captivate, H5P.
  • LMS: Moodle, Canvas, Cornerstone (SCORM/xAPI knowledge).
  • Design: Figma, Canva, Miro for storyboarding and prototyping.
  • Media: Camtasia, Audacity for video/audio production.

Portfolio Must-Haves

  • At least 2–3 sample projects with clear objectives, design process, and results.
  • Examples: onboarding microlearning module, branching scenario, job aid.
  • Showcase accessibility considerations and measurable impact.

Resume Tips

  • Highlight LXD skills and tools in an ATS-friendly format.
  • Use metrics: “Reduced onboarding time by 25% through microlearning design.”
  • Include relevant keywords: instructional design, SCORM, learning objectives, accessibility.

Interview Prep

  • Be ready to explain your design process from analysis to evaluation.
  • Have examples of how you measured learning effectiveness.
  • Prepare to discuss accessibility and learner engagement strategies.
  • Practice scenario design prompts.

Action Plan

  1. Learn the basics of LXD and core instructional models.
  2. Get hands-on with at least one authoring tool and one LMS.
  3. Build a small but polished portfolio of 2–3 projects.
  4. Update resume and LinkedIn with LXD focus.
  5. Apply for entry-level LXD or instructional designer roles.
